CONNECTICUT TRAFFIC STOP ANALYSIS

The dataset from Connecticut State Police traffic stops provides valuable insights into various aspects of traffic enforcement. The goal of the analysis is to answer specific questions related to the frequency and type of stops, violations, and outcomes, while considering demographic factors like gender, age, and race. Key KPIs: 1. Total Stops Made: The total number of traffic stops conducted. 2. Total Tickets Issued: How many tickets were issued during the stops. 3. Average Age of Drivers: The average age of drivers involved in traffic stops. 4. Total Arrests: The total number of arrests made during these stops. Key Questions Answered Using the Dashboard: Traffic Violations by Driver's Gender: How do traffic violations vary by driver gender, and how can filtering by factors such as driver gender, age, or race provide a more focused analysis of violation trends? Stop Outcomes by Age Brackets: What are the stop outcomes based on different age brackets, and how can apply filters such as driver gender or contraband presence provide a clearer view of outcomes across age groups? Monthly Stop Trends: How do stop frequencies vary across different months of the year, and how can filtering by characteristics like age or race influence the trends observed over the months? Violation Trends: What are the trends in different types of traffic violations, and how can apply filters for driver gender, age, or contraband presence reveal more specific violation patterns? Daily Stop Trends: How do stop frequencies vary across different days of the week, and how can filtering by demographic factors or contraband found provide deeper insights into daily stop trends?

Overall Summary: This analysis suggests that traffic enforcement patterns are influenced by a variety of factors such as driver demographics (age, gender), time of year, and day of the week. Key trends point to a high incidence of speeding violations, with male and younger drivers being more frequently involved in violations. Traffic stops are more common during peak months (e.g., March, October) and weekdays (especially Friday and Saturday), indicating that law enforcement activities are higher during these periods.
